Historical

  • Diwali: The holiday Diwali, otherwise known as Deepavali dates back more than 2,500 years. Diwali is associated with multiple stories in religious texts often having to do with good trumping evil.
  • Independence Day: Independence Day is celebrated on August 15. It started in 1947 and commemorates independence from the British Empire. British rule lasted 190 years and started in 1757.
  • Holi: The history of the holiday Holi dates back to Indian mythology. It’s based on the myth of Hiranyakashipu.
  • Republic Day: Indian Republic Day commemorates the enactment of the constitution in India. It happened on January 26, 1950. The constitution was made three years after India’s independence.
  • Republic Day: Gandhi’s existence is a holiday and it is on the day of the U.N.’s international day of Non-violence.
  • Dassehra: Dasserha celebrates the victory of Rama defeating the demon king Ravana. People submerge statues of the gods in water.
  • Eid: Eid-al-Fitr marks the end of Ramadan and is celebrated on the first three days of the Islamic calendar. Usually, there is a feast on this day. People give gifts and celebrate on Eid-al-Fitr and graves are visited along with official receptions. Eid-al-Adha marks the final result of the hajj rites at Mina. People sacrifice a ritually acceptable animal and divide the meat among themselves, neighbors, and the poor.
  • Ramadan: Ramadan is a month-long fasting period. It is a period of atonement and restraint. People do not eat after dawn or before dusk, but other things can be considered as breaking the fast. You can break the fast by doing unkind deeds and performing immoral activities.
