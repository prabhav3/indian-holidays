Economic

  • Clothing: On most Indian holidays, people buy new clothing. Clothes consist of embroidered button-down shirts and dresses called saris. Street vendors usually sell most amount of clothes during this time. From my experience, jewelry stores are usually packed.
  • Sankranti: During Makar Sankranti, people mark the harvest season. This is when most people decide to begin the harvest and they do so because of the superstition of the northward movement of the sun.
  • Diwali: On Diwali, people buy diyas (lamps) and firecrackers to celebrate. Diyas are believed to guide the goddess of wealth into homes and bring wealth to those she visits.
  • Dassehra: On Dasserha, people buy and construct a massive statue of the demon Ravana. On the holiday, they light up the statue and it burns to the ground, showing how they have won over the demon. This statue alone costs Rs 13 lakhs or around 1.4 million dollars.
